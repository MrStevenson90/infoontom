About Thomas Stevenson

This unique upbringing shaped my worldview from a young age, preparing me for a life filled with diverse experiences.

At my core, I am an artist. This perspective has guided me through various roles and industries. My career has swerved into finance and law which was a helpful stepping stone to understanding how to bring my bigger visions to life.

My artistic journey has some milestones: I've produced a feature film that premiered to a full house, Played Guitar on a recorded that was regularly played on the radio, and set up both a record label and a studio. It's been a fun journey.

My professional philosophy is founded on the principles of exploration, seeking challenges, and minimizing distractions. I'm a staunch believer in the 10,000 hours rule, advocating for intensive dedication to one's craft, which often means working late into the night and on weekends. My experiences have reinforced my belief that the USA, especially California (SF,LA), is the best place to make a meaningful impact on the world.

Currently, I am dedicated to "Llama v Unicorn," a project aimed at providing a social alternative to traditional gambling mechanisms like slot machines and simulated sports betting. By creating real sports leagues and offering minutely markets, this initiative seeks to engage predictive gamers in an innovative way.

Looking forward, my goal is to establish a business that uses sports probability as a gateway to financial education.

I share a simple, fulfilling life with my partner, Meaghan, and our dog, Brizzie, in the Bay Area. Together, we enjoy work and spending time outside in the summer.
